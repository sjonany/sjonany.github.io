# Hell Yeah or No - Derek Sivers [Book notes]
*Published 2026-05-06*

*Tags: [#book-notes](/tags/book-notes.html)*

### TL;DR
Useful mindsets to consider when figuring out what to work on in life. If it's a "hell yeah", then dive right in. No need to have remorse about the alternatives -- your life is long enough to have more of these rarer "hell yeah"'s.
### Applicable to me
New mindsets
- **Following stoke is optimal.** When you're stoked about something, just go all in and start the act now. Don't worry about the alternative good options, you'll get to it someday.
- **Share, unique imitation is good.** Share your work, even it's "already been done before." Your unique twist might make it useful to others - you don't know until you share it.
- **Decide late.** Decide / respond as late as possible. I usually err on the side of responding too quickly, and sometimes the work turns out to be unnecessary, or has to be redone because I don't have all the info anyway. So - if not urgent, just set a reminder to respond and think about later.

Actions
- **Possible futures.** Brainstorm list of possible futures. Keep a live document in monthly goals.
- **Inspiring goals.** For each project, pick a goal that makes you excited to act now.

### Notable quotes
#### Feeling better about the choice
**One thing for years.** Are you frustrated that the world wants you to pick one thing , because you want to do them all ? The problem is thinking short term — assuming that if you don't do all the things now , they won't happen . The solution is to think long term . Do just one thing for a few years , then another for a few years , then another . You can fully focus on one direction at a time , without feeling conflicted or distracted , because you know you'll get to the others .

**Imitation is ok, maybe even better.** Like a funhouse mirror that distorts what it reflects , your imitation will turn out much different from the original . Maybe even better. So look around at those existing ideas in the world . You can imitate them and still be offering something valuable and unique .
#### On trying hard

**Standard pace is for chumps.** He taught me that " the standard pace is for chumps " — that the system is designed so anyone can keep up . If you're more driven than most people , you can do way more than anyone expects .

**All in when stoked.** Then when something is extra - rewarding , it's time to switch strategies . Focus all of your energy on this one thing . Don't be leisurely . Strike while it's hot . Be a freak . Give it everything you've got . If by chance it was a dead - end road , then switch your strategy back to trying everything .

**Unnecessary stress.** When I notice that I'm all stressed out about something or driving myself to exhaustion, realize that half of my effort wasn't effort at all , but just unnecessary stress that made me feel like I was doing my best .

#### Choosing what to do
**Happy, smart, and useful.** There are three things to consider when making life - size decisions : What makes you happy, What's smart — meaning long - term good for you, What's useful to others. ... Happy and smart isn't bad . The self - focus feels great at first . But you can't actually pull yourself up by your bootstraps . Ultimately you must be **lifted by those around you .**

**Aim for unique and useful.** I'm glad my old confidence is gone because it thought I was right , and maybe even great , but not anymore . Now I aim to make my work — my little contribution to the world — just unique and useful .

 **A "Possible Futures" folder.** Then I made a little change that made a big difference : on my computer , I made a folder called " Possible Futures . " For each big plan , I make a new file in that folder and put all of my ideas and research into it .

**Pick one when ready.** When I finish a big project and I'm feeling ready for a new future , I open this folder and pick one to make real .

**Don't have to be local.** I'm not being as useful to the rest of the world . So I'm finally admitting : I'm not local . I feel equally connected to many places . Just because I live in one place now , that doesn't mean I should ignore the others . To me , the emphasis on local stuff never felt right .

**Think how it ends.** Before you start something , think of the ways it could end . Sometimes the smart choice is to say no to the whole game .

**Bad judges of own work.** Hit songwriters often admit that their most successful hit song was one they thought was just stupid , even not worth recording . We're clearly bad judges of our own creations . We should just put them out there and let the world decide .
#### Executing
**Great goals make you act now.** Judge a goal by how well it changes your actions in the present moment . A bad goal makes you say , " I want to do that someday . " A great goal makes you take action immediately. But unless it changes your actions , right now , it's not a great goal . Find another variation that excites you .

**Change needs empty space.** Personal change needs some space to happen . To bring something new into your life , you need somewhere to put it . If your current habits are filling your day , where are these new habits supposed to go ?

**Learning without doing is wasted.** Learning without doing is wasted . If I don't use what I learn , then it was pointless ! How horrible to waste those hundreds of hours I spent learning , and not turn it into action .
 
**Inspiration through your work.** Because nothing is truly inspiring unless you apply it to your work . ( " work " meaning your life's output , whether creative , business , or personal ) . In other words , your work , itself , is the inspiration .

**Decide as late as possible.** So when should you make decisions ? When you have the most information , when you're at your smartest : as late as possible . ... Resist the urge to figure it all out in advance . Realize that now , in the beginning , is when you know the least . When people expect you to make these decisions in advance , get used to saying , " We don't know yet ."
#### Balancing with life
**Love and money each half.** Do something for love and something for money . Don't try to make one thing satisfy your entire life . Each half of your life becomes a remedy for the other . You get paid stability for part of your day , but then need creative time for expression . So you push yourself creatively , expose your vulnerable art to the public , feel the frustration of rejection and apathy , and then long for some stability again . Each half is a remedy for the other .

**Don't taint love with money.** Don't taint something you love with the need to make money from it .
