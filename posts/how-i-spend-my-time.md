# How I spend my time
*Published 2022-04-18*

*Tags: [#life-reflection](/tags/life-reflection.html)*

I want my time spending to [match](https://dynalist.io/d/EENlNdcIlekLLazZ7LM1dMSJ#z=xxtdSpWoczK0Rwmir2S5x4pK) my [values](https://dynalist.io/d/IfWvgrSkDIZuFvikXF9Z_1_V#z=P3g7-S2ECqFXHr1zFiyN2XyI). I also want to tackle some [impossibles](https://dynalist.io/d/aGCih6s7z8Rbxu31v02Z3aYg) along the way while keeping my sanity. Here is a sketch of how I plan to spend my time.

**Summary** (See [this](https://dynalist.io/d/1mqCJXve99eM55aWBZ3To-Lm) for the detailed calendar)
- **My values.** Truth, Love, Play. See [here](https://dynalist.io/d/IfWvgrSkDIZuFvikXF9Z_1_V#z=P3g7-S2ECqFXHr1zFiyN2XyI) for more details.
- **Weekday** balance is 70% truth, 30% love / play. 25% of the truth-seeking activities are self-driven, outside of work. It is critical that my full time job is relevant to my truth value — beware of anything else [money, status, …] that chips away at the truth value proportion.
- **Weekend** balance is more flexible. Save mornings for deep work. Then the rest of the day is filled with more socials.

[**Calendar**](https://dynalist.io/d/1mqCJXve99eM55aWBZ3To-Lm#z=spykuiNHEUy7v38r0XXyj8V0) **breakdown.** Here are some note-worthy activities outside of the usual 40h work.
- **Morning** [**deep work**](https://dynalist.io/d/gX0Up701RVrqzi56Gl00R9MJ)**.** Mornings are for self-driven projects / learnings. This should **not** be related to paid work. It’s something that I want to do even if I retire.
- **Learning.** Make time to learn about (1) topics directly related to your work (2) topics adjacently related to your work, or the larger field (3) anything else that’s non-work related. Books / [Youtube](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCbfYPyITQ-7l4upoX8nvctg) are good for this.
- **Brainstorm.** Make time to brainstorm and think in [solitude](https://dynalist.io/d/kddDvlEHv7SXPJkaJMfh5Dh9#z=5crNwxqa06R7RPNanttiMf3k). Do this in a variety of enviroments: [walk, in a room, driving].
- **Play, socials.** Always end the day w/ play and socials. Batch up most of the socials to the weekends.

**On guilt.** Sometimes I wonder if I could do more. Here are some reflections to assuage my guilt.
- Reason 1: Desired ratio. Prior to creating the calendar, I already [planned for 70%](https://dynalist.io/d/1mqCJXve99eM55aWBZ3To-Lm#z=SjsW48WFAwJFku_DD7QsxokL) of my time to be spent on the truth value. I got almost exactly that ratio for the weekday. In a way, don’t feel guilty because this proportion is **optimal** as per value-based budgeting.
- Reason 2: Diminishing return. 70% is [enough](https://dynalist.io/d/ihfXrUtifnZ3o9-M2bIpRgwk#z=4JEePguwIDCuBwvZU-urQBBb). If I trade off 10% time from play / love to truth, I may be gaining very little productivity points for larger loss in happiness / wellbeing.
- Reason 3: Weekend to mentally reset. It’s good to have a shift in balance of time spending on the weekends. This may also help me have a different mindset at the start of the week v.s. middle of the week -> more creative.
- Reason 4: Avoid burnout. Pace yourself. End the night with play. You want to be productive and happy long term. This is a marathon, not a sprint.
- Reason 5: Grit training. Being gritty during recovery is part of [grit](https://dynalist.io/d/aGCih6s7z8Rbxu31v02Z3aYg#z=s2HDjOKouxaJsOneCAMcTAQL) training.
- Reason 6: Past experience. I have tried ~30h post-work studies prior to my [short stint](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/reflection-from-academia-back-industry-stephen-jonany/?trackingId=dBUWexQd%2FNu8DA2pC75%2BZQ%3D%3D) in comp neuro (after being inspired by [Nate Soares](https://mindingourway.com/the-mechanics-of-my-recent-productivity/)). That was fine for the short term, but I didn’t feel that it was sustainable, and felt bad that I’m not tending to my other values (especially socials). 20h should be enough.
